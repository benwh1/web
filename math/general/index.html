Let $\displaystyle{s(n)=\sum_{\tan(x)=x,x>0}\frac{1}{x^n}}$. Then \begin{align*} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}s(2n)x^{2n} &=\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{x^2\tan(x)}{x-\tan(x)}+3\right) \\ &=\frac{x^2}{10}+\frac{x^4}{350}+\frac{x^6}{7875}+\frac{37 x^8}{6063750}+\frac{59 x^{10}}{197071875}+\cdots \end{align*} Similarly, let $\displaystyle{s(n)=\sum_{\csc(x)=x,x>0}\frac{1}{x^n}}$. Then \begin{align*} \sum_{n=1}^{\infty}s(2n)x^{2n} &=\frac{x}{2}\left(\frac{1+x\cot(x)}{\csc(x)-x}\right) \\ &=x^2+\frac{2x^4}{3}+\frac{21x^6}{40}+\frac{59x^8}{140}+\frac{24625x^{10}}{72576}+\cdots \end{align*}
